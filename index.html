HOW TO AVOID FUTURE ATTACKS

In the image below we have highlighted areas for you to inspect in a future email.
A phishing email will often contain the name of someone you know, your boss or even a family member.
This doesn't mean they have their email address, but they randomly or purposly put a name you know as the sender.
Carefully look at the email address. Unless the sender recently changed their address, it should be the same as in previous mails.
If the sender is from another company, gmail will mark the mail with a yellow sign saying "external", which is another reason to be alert.
Lastly, if the sender has a different profile picture than normally, or the image with a question mark, double check if the person changed their profile picture.

Do's and Don'ts

Double check email addresses Don't trust an email just because the name is similar to one you know
Carefully read the email too see if it includes any links or downloads Don't trust the content unless you are 100% certain it is safe
Thou shalt flee immorality... and untrusted websites. If you clicked a link and notice anything suspicious, leave the page immediately Never enter a password in an unexpected popup website
Report phishing emails to Technology Services Don't be silent when noticing possible attacks on the organization. Others might walk in the trap you avoided
