HOW TO AVOID FUTURE ATTACKS

  • Observe and be suspicious to email addresses. Does the email address match to mails you have received in the past?
  • Be critical, a phishing email will often contain the name of someone you know, and in some cases(e.g if the person got hacked) the same email address.
  • The story, phishing emails usually tells a story that tries to trick you by giving you a time limit to recover a password or complete a payment.
  • Look for signs, If the sender is from another company, gmail will mark the email with a yellow sign saying "external". This is located next to the subject line.
  • New profile picture, if the sender has a different profile picture than normally, double check if the person changed their profile picture.
  • Protection is key, use multi-factor authentication when applicabale, use strong passwords and lock your device whenever its left unseen. Dangerous phishing schemes can be done if a hacker gets a hold of your email credentials

How to verify that a link is trusted

When we receive an email that includes a link, regardless of your trust in the sender, we always reccomend you verifying the link. How do we do this? We have to look at what is called the domain name. pbx.catchthefire.com is a safe link to a safe website. The part that says "catchthefire.com" is what we call the domain name of the website. "pbx" is what we call the prefix. We want to be aware of this before we click on a link. Another example of this is catchthefire.bamboohr.com. Bamboohr.com is the domain name, and catchthefire is the prefix. We are aware that bamboohr.com is safe and we can therefore proceed to click any links where bamboohr is displayed as the domain name. Anyhow, the prefix can be a stumbling block, as a hacker can make their website as catchthefire.dangerousdomain.com. "dangerousdomain" might be overlooked because the prefix (catchthefire) seems safe, but thats what conceales the trap. In any case where the domain name is unknown, we reccomend you search on google: is "dangerousdomain.com" a safe website?

We do a google search and it turns out "dangerousdomain.com" has been used by scammers previously. What do we do now? Firstly we want to report all phishing schemes to the manager of Technology Services. Furthermore you should inform your manager and your coworkers about the scheme, in order to prevent them from walking in the trap you avoided.
